The Boda Economy

How two wheels move a nation's money

Every day, over two hundred thousand motorcycle taxis carry Uganda's cash, credit, and gossip through streets no census ever fully counts. Immaculate N. Aber spent three years riding pillion to map the ledgers, loans, and mobile money flows that keep the boda economy running.

About Immaculate

IA
Immaculate N. Aber
Writer & independent economist

Immaculate spent three years on the back of boda-bodas across Kampala, Gulu, and Mbale, recording how riders and stage chairmen actually manage money — long before any bank or app noticed. She writes for a general reader, with the eye of someone who has counted the coins herself.

This is her first book. It is self-published, and every copy sold goes straight to funding the next one.
